Module 02: Phishing & Social Engineering

Learning Objectives

Passive watching doesn't build muscle memory. I simulated a high-stakes situation that empowered users to explore failure in a safe environment. Leveraging the Scenarios game from The Training Arcade, I created a branching game where users could explore and understand the consequences of their choices without risking real-world assets. Participation was incentivized with the opportunity to be selected to join the Security Team in creating a phishing simulation which created a unique opportunity to breakdown the all to common "us vs. them" dynamic between security teams and the rest of the organization while providing the winner with an adversary mindset.

  • By designing a phish, the winner gains a deep understanding of the "Urgency Traps" and "Close-Enough" sender tactics outlined in Company Policy.
  • They learn to think like an adversary, which naturally sharpens their ability to spot red flags in their own inbox—transforming them from a potential target into a high-functioning "human sensor".
  • It provides a rare, legal opportunity to "hack" their boss or coworkers, replacing the usual anxiety of security training with a sense of fun and agency.
  • Technical professionals often struggle to write phishes that sound like real Sales or Marketing emails. A Guest Villain from those departments provides the "intel" needed to create more realistic, high-fidelity simulations.
  • This collaboration breaks down the "us vs. them" barrier. It shifts the perception of the Security Team from an enforcement only body to a group of peers who work alongside the staff to "fix the incident together".
  • Working with a non-technical peer gives the Security Team direct insight into how employees perceive risks, allowing us to refine our Incident Management response templates based on actual human psychology.
