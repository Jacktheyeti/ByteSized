Module 02: Phishing & Social Engineering

Learning Objectives

Passive watching doesn't build muscle memory and lately, phishing simulations were starting to "feel like that". I wanted to simulate a 'high-stakes situation that empowered users to explore failure in a safe environment. Leveraging Scenarios from The Training Arcade, I curated a branching game where users could explore and understand the consequences of their choices without risking real-world assets. The learning didn't stop there because a winner was randomly selected to join the security team in designing and executing the phishing simulation.

  • dentify Sophisticated Red Flags: Train users to look past the subject line to detect "Urgency Traps," "Close-Enough" spoofed domains, and generic greetings designed to manipulate behavior.
  • Apply Technical Validation Skills: Instill the habit of the "Hover Test" and sender verification, ensuring team members can differentiate between legitimate ELB communications and malicious redirects.
  • Navigate Role-Specific Threats: Provide a deep dive into department-specific social engineering (e.g., "Whaling" for Sam the Builder or "Supply Chain" attacks for Alex the Connector) to ensure the training feels relevant to their actual daily workflow.
  • Execute Incident Response Protocols: Reinforce the exact steps for immediate remediation following a suspected click, emphasizing transparency and reporting over silent panic.
