Module 01: Security Training Sucks. We're Fixing it.

Learning Objectives

Security is a lifestyle, not a lunch-and-learn. My goal with the first module was to bridge the gap between home safety and office security, thereby lowering the barrier to entry for employee engagement. Not only was there 110% engagement rate with the module compared to the training last year, but many team member reached out exclaiming they were 'actually excited' to see what was next and that they 'actually learned something valuable'.

  • Establish Foundational Hygiene: Empower team members to implement baseline security habits, such as identifying password reuse and adopting professional password management tools.
  • Master Physical and Digital Access: Standardize the habit of securing workstations through screen locking and environmental awareness, ensuring the "Least Privilege" principle begins at the desk.
  • Proactive Lifecycle Management: Shift the responsibility of system maintenance to the user by emphasizing the importance of immediate OS patching and local data cleanup.
  • Cultivate Reporting Instincts: Neutralize the fear associated with reporting by framing the "Security Snitch" as a vital team player who protects the collective from cover-up risks.

Module Specs

Duration: 4 mins

Format: Interactive Checklist

Engagement Rate: 110%
